FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an account to generate a proposal?

You need a free account to generate a full proposal. The SBIR Blueprint and Prompt Pack do not require an account.

What is the difference between Starter and Pro?

Starter generates your proposal in one pass at $149. Pro runs two critique-refine iterations — the AI reviews its own output and rewrites each section to improve quality — at $449. Done-With-You adds a personal review by Tom Erickson at $1,200.

Does this work for DoD and NASA, not just NSF?

Yes. Grantentic generates proposals formatted for NSF, DoD, and NASA SBIR programs. Each agency has different section requirements, scoring weights, and language expectations. We apply the correct rubric automatically when you select your agency.

What happens if a section exceeds the character limit?

We enforce hard character limits in code, not just in the prompt. If the AI generates content over the limit, it is automatically truncated at the last complete sentence before the limit. The quality report shows the character count for every section.

Is my company data and technology description secure?

Your data is stored in a private Supabase database row keyed to your account. No other user can see your company information or proposals. We do not sell or share your data. Read our full privacy policy for details.

What is the anti-fabrication guarantee?

Grantentic never invents team members, degrees, universities, employers, publications, letters of intent, or customer conversations. If we cannot substantiate a detail from your intake form, we emit a bracketed placeholder like [specify radiation tolerance target] instead of a plausible-sounding fabrication. This protects you from submitting false information to a federal agency.

How is Grantentic different from Rogue or other proposal tools?

Most proposal tools are built for government contractors writing RFP responses. Grantentic is built specifically for SBIR Phase I applicants — early-stage deep tech founders who need to pass NSF's intellectual merit review, not win a procurement. We enforce NSF's own seven funding criteria, detect fabrications in real time, and enforce exact character limits that the NSF submission system requires.
