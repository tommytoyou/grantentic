The Problem

Why Most SBIR Proposals Fail

Federal reviewers spend 15–20 minutes per proposal. Most get a "Fair" rating within the first paragraph and never recover.

Rejected in the first paragraph

Vague problem statements, systems-integration framing instead of high-risk R&D, and missing testable hypotheses earn a "Fair" before the reviewer reaches page two.

Each agency scores differently

NSF weights intellectual merit at 50%. DoD weights technical merit at 40%. NASA weights mission alignment at 35%. A one-size-fits-all draft loses on every rubric.

Consultants cost $5,000–$15,000

Traditional SBIR consultants charge mid-five figures per proposal. Most of those proposals still get rejected — and the consultant keeps the check.
