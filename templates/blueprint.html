1. The Problem

★★★★★ Critical

50% of reviewer score

Reviewers decide in the first paragraph whether your proposal is worth reading. You must quantify the problem, show who suffers, and prove current solutions are inadequate.

"Proposal does not clearly articulate the problem or its significance."

2. Technical Approach

★★★★★ Critical

25% of reviewer score

Show exactly what is technically new about your approach. Reviewers are domain experts — they spot hand-waving immediately. Be specific about your methodology.

"The proposal describes an incremental improvement rather than a genuinely novel technical approach."

3. Competitive Landscape

★★★★★ Important

25% of reviewer score

Name real competitors or alternatives. Claiming "no competitors exist" is a red flag. Reviewers want bottom-up market sizing, not top-down analyst reports.

"No competitive analysis provided. Claiming no competitors exist is not credible."

4. Team

★★★★★ Supporting

Included in overall assessment

Reviewers ask "Can this team actually do what they claim?" Connect each person's background directly to the project needs. Prior SBIR success is especially strong.

"Team lacks specific expertise required for the proposed technical approach."
