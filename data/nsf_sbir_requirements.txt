NSF SBIR Phase I Grant Requirements and Evaluation Criteria

PROGRAM OVERVIEW:
The NSF Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I program provides funding up to $275,000 for 6-12 months to establish technical feasibility and commercial potential of innovative technologies.

PHASE I OBJECTIVES:
1. Demonstrate scientific and technical merit
2. Establish feasibility of proposed innovation
3. Show potential for commercial application
4. Demonstrate team capability to execute

- U.S.-based and majority U.S.-owned
- Principal Investigator primarily employed by company
- Research must be performed in the United States

EVALUATION CRITERIA (Weighted equally):

1. INTELLECTUAL MERIT
   - Soundness of technical approach
   - Innovation and originality
   - Qualifications of team
   - Adequacy of resources
   - Clear technical objectives and feasibility plan
   - Novelty compared to existing solutions
   - Scientific/technical risk and mitigation strategies

2. BROADER IMPACTS
   - Potential to benefit society
   - Advancement of discovery and understanding
   - Broadening participation of underrepresented groups
   - Enhancement of research infrastructure
   - Benefits beyond science and engineering
   - Societal impact of successful commercialization

3. COMMERCIAL IMPACT
   - Market opportunity size and accessibility
   - Clear path to revenue and profitability
   - Competitive advantage and barriers to entry
   - Go-to-market strategy
   - Customer validation and letters of support
   - Realistic business model
   - Economic impact potential
   - Scalability of solution

4. TEAM QUALIFICATIONS
   - Relevant technical expertise
   - Track record of execution
   - Commercialization experience
   - Advisors and partnerships
   - Diversity and complementary skills

REQUIRED PROPOSAL SECTIONS:

PROJECT PITCH (1-2 pages):
- Clear problem statement
- Proposed solution and innovation
- Market opportunity
- Competitive landscape
- Team overview
- Phase I objectives and success criteria

TECHNICAL OBJECTIVES (5-6 pages):
- Detailed technical approach
- Innovation and scientific merit
- Current state of technology (TRL level)
- Phase I research plan and methodology
- Key technical risks and mitigation
- Success metrics and validation approach
- Timeline and milestones
- Path to Phase II

BROADER IMPACTS (1-2 pages):
- Societal benefits beyond commercial success
- Scientific/technical knowledge advancement
- Educational opportunities
- Diversity and inclusion efforts
- Environmental sustainability
- Infrastructure improvements
- Ethical considerations

COMMERCIALIZATION PLAN (2-3 pages):
- Market analysis and size
- Target customers and value proposition
- Competitive analysis
- Business model and revenue streams
- Go-to-market strategy
- Customer discovery and validation
- Intellectual property strategy
- Team capability for commercialization
- Financial projections
- Path beyond Phase I funding

QUALITY STANDARDS:

Writing Quality:
- Clear, concise, and well-organized
- Free of jargon; accessible to non-specialist reviewers
- Strong, compelling narrative
- Evidence-based claims with citations where appropriate
- Logical flow between sections

Technical Quality:
- Specific, measurable objectives
- Realistic timeline and milestones
- Appropriate methodology
- Risk awareness and mitigation plans
- Clear success criteria
- Demonstrates deep domain expertise

Commercial Quality:
- Market-validated opportunity
- Realistic financial projections
- Clear competitive differentiation
- Evidence of customer interest
- Viable business model
- Scalable approach

Common Weaknesses to Avoid:
- Overly ambitious scope for Phase I
- Insufficient technical detail
- Weak commercial validation
- Unclear success metrics
- Generic market analysis
- Missing risk mitigation
- Poor team-technology fit
- Lack of customer evidence
- Unrealistic timelines
- Ignoring competitors

Success Factors:
- Focus on feasibility demonstration
- Clear Phase I deliverables
- Strong preliminary data
- Customer letters of support
- Well-defined market niche
- Realistic resource allocation
- Team expertise alignment
- Clear IP strategy
- Measurable milestones
- Compelling societal benefit
